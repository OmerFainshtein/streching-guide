Welcome to your new strtching guide

My name is Omer Fainshtein and I have been an amateur triathlete for about 5 years


During my training, it often happened that a not very common muscle getting sore and I had to spend time searching websites to find exercises that would be relevant to this specific muscle


Following this - I developed a website that provides information and exact exercises for the muscle you choose

So, how does it work?

With the 360 model of the human body found down here
you can visually choose the specific area you want to focus on today
Within one click you will immediately receive a 3 exercises and instractions related to the specific muscle you have chosen
thus you can achieve an efficient, effective and easy-to-use stretching workout.

lets get start!

  1. With the help of the mouse - rotate the model as you wish

  2. Below the model there is a link to each of the numbered muscles
    click on the muscle number you want to focus

  3. Do the exercises that are adapted to the area you have chosen

  4. you can easly return to model by clicking 'the model' on the navigation bar

Soleus

This exercise is best done on a step. Stand with the ball of your foot on the step. Keep your knees straight, and do not bend them! You will need something to hold onto keep your balance, like a rail, or a wall, or the back of a chair. Let your heels drop until a stretch is felt at the back of the lower leg, and behind the knees. Hold the stretch for 10-15 seconds.


repeat this exercise 1-3 times per day

Sit on the floor with your out in front of you. Wrap the towel around the ball of your foot just below your toes. Gently pull on the towel, allowing your foot to slowly bend up toward your knee while keeping your knee straight. You should feel a slight stretching feeling in the back of your leg. Hold the stretched position for 6 counts, then slowly release and repeat.

repeat this exercise 15 times per day.

Stand facing a wall from 3 feet away. Take one step toward the wall with your right foot. Place both palms on the wall. Bend both knees and lean forward. Keep both heels on the floor. Hold for 30 to 60 seconds. Then relax both legs. Repeat the exercise 2 times. Switch legs and repeat.


Repeat this exercise 3 times a day, or as instructed.

Tibialis anterior

This stretch is similar to the lying quadriceps stretch. Lie on your one side. Now, bend your upper knee so your foot is behind your back. Reach back and grasp your forefoot, pulling it towards your back. Hold for 10 to 20 seconds.







Repeat for both feet.

Get into a kneeling position. Keep your feet hip-width apart, and your toes turned slightly inward. Keep your back straight, and shoulder blades slightly pinched together. Keep your neck neutral. Sit back on the heels of your feet.






Hold this position for about 30 seconds. Rest and repeat.

Stand tall. You need to use a hand on a wall or other support for balance. Slightly bend your both knees. The right foot remains squarely on the floor. The foot to be stretched or left foot is placed just behind this stable/right foot, with the toe of the left foot touching the floor. Keeping your toe firmly on the floor, pull the left leg forward so you feel a stretch from the top of your left foot through your shins. Once you feel a stretch, hold it for 10 to 30 seconds.

Repeat the stretch with the right foot.

Hamstrings

Every few repetitions, hold the stretch for a bit and see where you’re at.

Lie on the floor near the outer corner of a wall or a door frame. Raise your left leg and rest your left heel against the wall. Keep your left knee slightly bent. Gently straighten your left leg until you feel a stretch along the back of your left thigh. Hold for about 30 seconds.

Quadriceps

Let your upper body descend backwards and rest on your hands, arms outstretched. Bring the hips forward as high as possible and feel the stretch in the front of the thighs.







Hold the position for 5 seconds

Begin lying on your front. Bend one leg toward your body, grasp it with your hand, and pull it further until you feel a stretch in the front of your thigh and hold.





Make sure to continue breathing evenly during the stretch. Do not arch your low back.

Sit with your right leg bent, with your right heel just to the outside of your right hip. The left leg is bent and the sole of your left foot is next to the inside of your upper right leg. (Try not to let your right foot flare out to the side in this position.) Now slowly lean straight back until you feel an easy stretch in your right quadriceps. Use your hands for balance and support. Hold an easy stretch for 30 seconds. Do not hold any stretches that are painful.

Abductor

Sit on the floor with your side against the wall. Lift your legs up onto the wall as you roll over onto your back. With your buttocks touching the wall, spread your legs apart, resting on the wall. Place your arms where it is most comfortable.



Hold for 10-30 seconds.

Sit on the floor. Put the soles of your feet together so your knees are pointed outward. Pull your heels in toward your groin, as close as is comfortable. Put your hands on your knees, and gently push them closer to the floor. Hold for 30 to 60 seconds. Relax and repeat 2 times, or as instructed.

Repeat this exercise 3 times a day

Serratus

Hold onto something above you. Bring your arm and shoulder blades backwards. Allow your body to sink down. Keep your shoulder completely relaxed.







Hold for 30 seconds.

Place your hand onto a wall at shoulder height. Assume a lunge position. Pull your shoulder blade backwards. Lean your body weight into the arm that is on the wall. Turn your torso away from this side.






Hold for 30 seconds.

Sit sideways on the floor. Place your hand on the floor towards the side of you. Keep this arm completely straight throughout this stretch. Sink your body weight onto this arm. Make sure to keep your shoulder completely relaxed. Bow your torso towards this side. Lift your hips slightly off the ground as you place more of your body weight onto the arm.

Hold for 30 seconds.

Abdominal Muscles

Start slowly and stretch your abs with caution. It’s very important that you don’t feel any pain while stretching. As you gain strength and flexibility, you’ll be able to stretch further.







Hold the stretch for 20 to 30 seconds and then release

Stand straight with your feet hip-width apart and look directly ahead of you. Lift one arm up above your head and slowly bend sideways while reaching down with the opposite hand, until you feel a stretch at the side of your trunk. Maintain the stretch without twisting or leaning forward. Slowly return to initial position. For more support, you can stand next to a wall so your outstretched hand can use the wall as extra support.

Hold the stretch for 20-30 seconds.

Sit down on a chair with your spine straight. Rotate your body to one side and hold the position. Can use the chair to help hold the position. **Don’t push yourself into pain.









Hold the stretch for 20-30 seconds.

Latissimus dorsi

Reach your hand down towards the middle back region. Place your other hand onto the elbow of the hand that is reaching down the back. Pull the elbow towards the midline. Tilt your torso towards the side.









Hold for 30 seconds.

Start in a crawling position. Sit your buttocks on top of your ankles. Stretch out both arms and place them in front of you as far away as possible. Keep your lower back rounded.







Hold for 30 seconds. To stretch one Latissimus Dorsi at a time, reach out both arms to the opposite side.

** This exercise requires proper equipment

Make sure you know the exact location of the Latissimus Dorsi muscle. Lie down on your side. Place the Latissimus Dorsi muscle directly on top of a foam roller. Position your arm in an outstretched position. Apply as much body weight onto the foam roller as tolerated. Keep relaxed. Do not tense up. Make sure to cover the entire length of the muscle.

Continue for 2 minutes.

Deltoid

Hold a towel in your right hand and flex your right elbow with your right hand by your ear. Reach behind with the left hand and grip the towel as far up as your can. Pull up with the right hand and down with the left to create the stretch.


Do for 30 seconds and repeat on the other side.

Kneel down by bending your knees and sitting on your feet. Lie your torso over your thighs and with both arms lying above your head. Bring the left arm underneath and across your body while reaching to the right. Feel the stretch in your rear deltoids and lats.

Hold for 60 seconds and repeat on the other side.

Stand up straight and bring the left arm behind you with its elbow bent to 90 degrees. Place your left hand on your spine with fingers pointing up to the ceiling. Use your right hand to support your forearm. Repeat on the other side.



30 seconds per side works well here.
